About Me

Information is only usable if it is retrievable. I am interested in how to build user-centric collections, systems, and experiences that promote discoverability and information literacy.

I am pursuing my master's degree in library and information science (MLIS) at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), where my studies focus on metadata, information architecture, and description. My MLIS portfolio showcases academic and professional work from my time at UCLA.

I am seeking opportunities that place user-centered design and information-seeking behavior first. My interests include digital librarianship, digital asset management, content strategy, and knowledge management.

Resume

I have had the opportunity to work in diverse settings with different information resources, including an academic library, policy institute, art museum, and community archive. Throughout all of these experiences, I gained a better understanding of recurrent information management issues, and developed my key strengths:

Metadata Creation

Supported mass metadata creation for thousands of digital assets at the UCLA Digital Library Program, automatically generating metadata with Python. Remediated metadata, ensuring consistent use of controlled vocabularies.

Processed collections and created finding aids on ArchivesSpace at Go For Broke National Education Center.

Metadata Governance

Defined and documented data content standards and remediation workflows at the Getty Research Institute. Used OpenRefine to clean legacy metadata to these standards.

Translated and integrated Italian terms into the Art and Architecture Thesaurus, transating scope notes and advising on placement within the hierarchies.

User Experience

Tested websites for usability at the UCLA Digital Library Program, recommending improvements in search and design.

Designed a data request survey at the Schaeffer Center for Health Policy & Economics, transforming a complex process involving several forms into a streamlined survey structured to ensure accurate and consistent data collection.

Project Management

Mapped my work within the larger project through workflow diagrams and documentation at the Getty Research Institute.

Weighed priorities and capacity to strategically determine which projects to prioritize at Go For Broke National Education Center.

Reference

Helped users navigate collections and request materials at UCLA Library Special Collections. Drafted a detailed technical manual to help users access born-digital and digitized materials.

Researched reference technologies and workflows, advising on integration into the library's practices.

Instruction

Developed and led tours for K-12 students at the Fowler Museum.

Supported instruction at UCLA Library Special Collections, including paging and day-of logistics. Planned an instruction session, conducting outreach and developing the lesson.
